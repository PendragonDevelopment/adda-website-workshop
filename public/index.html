Ancient history? Not really. A recently patched flaw in the JSON gem would let an attacker max out your ram by creating millions of Ruby symbols. Symbols are never garbage-collected.

These take advantage of a particular weakness in your operating system, application stack, etc. to fill up your server's memory, peg your CPU, etc. For example, the classic SYN flood attack works by sending SYN requests to a server. The server opens a ton of connections which aren't closed. With enough open connections, the OS will run out of RAM

Incognito tabs count, right?

Incognito tabs or private tabs only disables extensions and clears your browsers cookies at the end of your session. Websites (ie, Facebook) can still track your movement, and your browser can still track your bookmarks and general settings.
